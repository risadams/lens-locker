# Research: Windows Codec Sourcing and Licensing

Ticket: [../tickets/013-research-codec-licensing.md](../tickets/013-research-codec-licensing.md)
Status: findings complete, open questions flagged for decision (feeds
[014-v1-format-matrix](../tickets/014-v1-format-matrix.md)).

**Not legal advice.** This is engineering-facing research to inform a build decision.
Every row marked "real legal review needed" below should get one before v1 ships,
especially HEIC/HEIF and camera RAW.

## 1. Summary — Decision Table

| Family | Recommendation | Risk note |
|---|---|---|
| **HEIC/HEIF** | **User-supplied codec.** Call Windows' own HEIF/HEVC system codec (WIC `GUID_ContainerFormatHeif` + installed HEVC Video Extensions) when present; if absent, import metadata/thumbnail-only (if a thumbnail is embedded) or refuse and tell the user to install the free/OEM Microsoft extension. Do not bundle libheif+libde265 or any other HEVC decoder, in C or Rust. | HEVC patent pools (MPEG LA, Access Advance, Velos Media, plus holders outside any pool) create real royalty/infringement exposure for shipping *any* HEVC decoder — the risk is in the algorithm, not the implementation language or libheif's own clean LGPL-3.0 license. **Real legal review needed.** |
| **Camera RAW** | **Bundle, pure Rust: `rawler`** (LGPL-2.1, actively maintained, has CR3). Treat LibRaw (C, LGPL-2.1/CDDL dual) as a fallback-behind-feature-flag only if rawler's coverage proves insufficient, and only under the CDDL option. | rawloader is stale (last publish April 2022) and lacks CR3; rawler is pre-1.0/non-SemVer. LibRaw under LGPL-2.1 imposes relink obligations that conflict with a single static exe; the CDDL option avoids that but needs sign-off on attribution terms. |
| **AVIF** | **Safe to bundle**, but requires a deliberate Cargo choice: the `image` crate's default `avif` feature is **encode-only** (pure-Rust `ravif`). Decode needs either `avif-native` (C `dav1d`, needs meson/ninja/vcpkg on Windows) or a `[patch]` onto `re_rav1d`/`rav1d` (pure-Rust fork, API-compatible with the `dav1d` crate) for a fully static, no-C-toolchain build. | AV1 is royalty-free under AOM's *reciprocal* patent grant — a grant, not proof of "no patents." No copyleft anywhere in the stack once C `dav1d` is swapped for `rav1d`. |
| **JPEG XL** | **Safe to bundle for decode** (`jxl-oxide`, pure Rust, MIT/Apache-2.0, spec-conforming). **No safe encoder yet** — production-grade JXL encoding (incl. lossless-JPEG reconstruction, covered in ticket 008) only exists via GPL-3.0 `jpegxl-rs` or the libjxl C++ CLI. | Royalty-free per Google/Cloudinary Type-1 ISO declarations (a grant, same caveat as AV1). libjxl itself is pre-1.0 (v0.12.0). Decode-only via jxl-oxide is low-risk; don't add a JXL encoder without revisiting 008's open questions. |
| **SVG** | **Safe to bundle.** `resvg` + `usvg` + `tiny-skia` + `fontdb`, Apache-2.0 OR MIT, pure Rust, no non-Rust code in the final binary. | None identified. |

## 2. HEIC/HEIF

**libheif's own license is clean.** libheif is LGPL-3.0 (sample apps/language bindings MIT). Source: [libheif COPYING](https://github.com/strukturag/libheif/blob/master/COPYING), [strukturag/libheif](https://github.com/strukturag/libheif). Its usual HEVC decoder, **libde265, is LGPL-3.0** too. Source: [libde265.org](https://www.libde265.org/). So the *copyright* license story for the C stack is fine on paper. The problem is patents, which no copyright license addresses.

**The patent landscape is genuinely fragmented — three pools, plus holders outside all of them:**
- **MPEG LA's HEVC pool**: royalty applies to "HEVC Software Sold separately from a device" and preloaded pay-to-use software; per public reporting the first 100,000 units are royalty-free, then ~$0.20/unit up to a $25M annual cap. Source (secondary, mpegla.com itself was unreachable during this research pass — flagged as a gap): [design-reuse.com, MPEG LA HEVC license terms](https://www.design-reuse.com/news/202524742-mpeg-la-announces-license-terms-for-high-efficiency-video-coding-hevc-h-265-/), [streaminglearningcenter.com royalty summary PDF](https://streaminglearningcenter.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/03/HEVC_H264_DASH_Royalties-1.pdf).
- **Access Advance (formerly HEVC Advance)**: its own FAQ gives no blanket free-software exemption — "In general, HEVC software downloaded by users requires a license. However, there are some situations wherein a license is not needed" and directs vendors to contact them case-by-case. Source: [accessadvance.com/faq](https://accessadvance.com/faq/). Separately, its November 2016 policy revision waives royalties specifically for "application layer software downloaded to mobile devices or personal computers **after the initial sale of the device**, where the HEVC encoding or decoding is fully executed in software on a general purpose CPU" (browsers, media players, "various software applications") — "subject to certain exceptions/conditions" it doesn't enumerate. Source: [PR Newswire, HEVC Advance Announces Royalty-Free HEVC Software](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/hevc-advance-announces-royalty-free-hevc-software-300367212.html). Critically, **this exemption binds only Access Advance** — it explicitly does not shield a vendor from MPEG LA, Technicolor, or any other HEVC IP holder. Source: same PR Newswire release, corroborated by [Beamr Blog analysis](https://blog.beamr.com/2016/11/30/patent-pool-hevc-advance-responds-announces-royalty-free-hevc-software/).
- **Velos Media** and unpooled holders exist outside both consolidated pools; no blanket statement from either covers them.

**Pure Rust does not solve this — it's the same patents.** `imazen/heic` (pure-Rust HEIC/HEIF decoder, its own from-scratch HEVC I-slice implementation, passes 49/49 ITU-T HEVC conformance vectors, "Production" per its own README) states this directly: "HEVC (H.265) and its use in HEIF containers may be covered by patents held by third parties, including members of the Access Advance HEVC patent pool... Imazen holds no patents — only copyrights on the code we wrote." It is dual-licensed **AGPL-3.0 or commercial** (no free permissive option), which is itself a second reason it doesn't fit LensLocker's bundling posture. Source: [github.com/imazen/heic](https://github.com/imazen/heic). This library is notable for one more reason: it ships an **optional Windows Media Foundation backend** — i.e., it already implements the "call the OS codec instead of bundling one" pattern this research recommends.

**How comparable open-source viewers actually handle it:**
- **darktable**: HEIF import via libheif is an *optional* dependency ("libheif 1.13.0 (for HEIF import & export...)" — [darktable README](https://github.com/darktable-org/darktable/blob/master/README.md)), and a darktable maintainer said plainly during the original HEIF PR review: **"As HEVC is a patent minefield, Linux distributions build libheif only with AVIF support."** — cryptomilk, [darktable PR #8800](https://github.com/darktable-org/darktable/pull/8800). This is exactly the pattern of shipping libheif for the patent-clean AVIF path while treating HEVC decode as something the *distro/user* opts into, not something the project bundles.
- **IrfanView**: HEIC support ships as a separate, user-installed **plugin** (the PlugIns package), not bundled in the base installer. Source: [irfanview.com/plugins.htm](https://www.irfanview.com/plugins.htm) (exact plugin licensing terms weren't independently verified — flagged as a gap).
- **ImageGlass**: advertises native HEIC support but forum/support threads still point users at installing the **Windows HEIF Image Extensions / HEVC Video Extensions** for full decode — i.e., it leans on the OS codec rather than a bundled one in practice. Source: [ImageGlass supported formats](https://imageglass.org/docs/supported-formats), [ImageGlass Google Groups HEIC thumbnails thread](https://groups.google.com/g/imageglass/c/VKzCOUjU0FA).
- **XnView MP**: markets "no plugin or external codec required" HEIC support, i.e. it does bundle a decoder — this is the one surveyed app that appears to take on the patent exposure directly rather than delegate to the OS. Source: [xnview.com HEIC viewer page](https://www.xnview.com/en/heic-viewer/) (XnView's own licensing/royalty position was not found in a primary statement — flagged as a gap; XnView MP is not open source, so its risk calculus may differ from LensLocker's).

**Can Windows' own HEVC codec be used offline, without LensLocker bundling anything?** Yes, architecturally. Windows exposes HEIF/HEVC decode through **WIC** (`GUID_ContainerFormatHeif`) once the user has the **HEIF Image Extensions** (container parsing) and **HEVC Video Extensions** (the actual HEVC decode, free-from-OEM on most preinstalled Windows PCs, or a small paid Store item otherwise) installed. Source: [Microsoft Learn, HEIF extension codec](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/windows/win32/wic/heif-codec), [windows-rs `GUID_ContainerFormatHeif` docs](https://microsoft.github.io/windows-docs-rs/doc/windows/Win32/Graphics/Imaging/constant.GUID_ContainerFormatHeif.html). This is reachable from Rust via the `windows` crate's `Win32_Graphics_Imaging` feature (COM/WIC bindings), calling `IWICImagingFactory` the same way any native Win32 app would. Once installed, support is system-wide and works fully offline — no network call is needed at *decode* time, satisfying LensLocker's offline constraint, and the patent liability sits with Microsoft/the OEM (who already pay into the pools), not with LensLocker. There is **no supported offline-redistributable package** for HEIF Image Extensions comparable to the HEVC Video Extensions' "from device manufacturer" package, so LensLocker genuinely cannot bundle this even if it wanted to — it must detect and delegate, or degrade gracefully. Source: [Microsoft Q&A, HEIF Image Extensions Store restriction](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-ca/answers/questions/5853241/unable-to-install-heif-image-extensions-(heic-code).

**libheif-rs on Windows — practical build state.** The Rust bindings (`libheif-sys`/`libheif-rs`, maintained by Cykooz, latest `libheif-sys` 5.3.0 as of May 2026) locate libheif via **vcpkg** on Windows, or can compile libheif from embedded sources under an `embedded-libheif` feature for static linking — but that feature's own docs warn "the statically linked version of libheif doesn't have [its] dependencies, such as libde265, libaom and other" statically linked, i.e. true single-binary static linking still isn't turnkey. A fully static build needs `RUSTFLAGS=-Ctarget-feature=+crt-static` against vcpkg's `x64-windows-static` triplet. Source: [Cykooz/libheif-sys](https://github.com/Cykooz/libheif-sys), [0xc0000054, Building libheif on Windows with vcpkg](https://0xc0000054.github.io/libheif-sharp/libheif_windows_build_vcpkg.html). This is buildable, but it's a real C toolchain dependency (vcpkg + a C/C++ compiler) on every dev/CI machine — a meaningfully heavier lift than LensLocker's otherwise pure-Rust stack, on top of the patent question above.

## 3. Camera RAW

**LibRaw's license.** LibRaw is offered **dual/triple-licensed**: LGPL-2.1, or CDDL-1.0, or (historically) a proprietary "LibRaw own license" that the project's own site describes as being discontinued starting around v0.18, leaving LGPL-2.1 and CDDL-1.0 as the live options. Source: [libraw.org/node/2228, Libraw-Licensing](https://www.libraw.org/node/2228).

**Static vs. dynamic linking implications for a single static Rust exe:**
- **LGPL-2.1 mode**: "allows use in Commercial App if you use Library (LibRaw in this case) as shared-library and has published in open-source (under LGPL2) any changes you made to Library source. If you use LibRaw unmodified, you only need to use it in shared-library form (.DLL/.so/.dylib)." (quoted from the LibRaw licensing page above). In plain terms: LGPL-2.1 wants the library to remain independently replaceable by the end user, which a monolithic static-linked `.exe` defeats unless LensLocker also ships relinkable object files or otherwise satisfies LGPL's linking exception — friction against the "single static binary, no downloads" design goal.
- **CDDL-1.0 mode**: file-level weak copyleft. General CDDL analysis: "copyleft requirements apply on file level with no requirement that the library must be easy to modify or replace, making static linking not a problem" for CDDL (and similarly-structured licenses like EPL/MPL). Source: [FOSSA, Open Source Licenses 101: The CDDL](https://fossa.com/blog/open-source-licenses-101-cddl-common-development-distribution-license/); general weak-copyleft/static-linking analysis corroborated across multiple secondary sources. Practically: choosing LibRaw's **CDDL option** and statically linking it into LensLocker's binary, with attribution retained, is the linking-compatible path if LibRaw is used at all. (LibRaw's own page: "Attribution is needed in both cases. Written/signed form is not needed.")
- A Rust binding exists (`rsraw`, "a comprehensive Rust wrapper for the LibRaw library... works on Linux/Windows/MacOS/iOS/Android") if this path is pursued. Source: [Hexilee/rsraw](https://github.com/Hexilee/rsraw).

**Pure-Rust alternatives:**
- **`rawloader`** (Pedro Côrte-Real). LGPL-2.1. Format coverage per its own README: "Minolta MRW, Sony ARW, SRF and SR2, Mamiya MEF, Olympus ORF, Samsung SRW, Epson ERF, Kodak KDC, DCS and DCR, Panasonic RW2, Fuji RAF, Adobe DNG, Pentax PEF, Canon CRW and CR2, Leaf IIQ and MOS, Hasselblad 3FR, Nikon NRW and NEF, ARRI ARI" — notably **no Canon CR3** (Canon's current format since ~2018). Source: [pedrocr/rawloader](https://github.com/pedrocr/rawloader). **Last published version 0.37.1, April 15, 2022** — effectively unmaintained for ~4 years as of this research. Source: [lib.rs/crates/rawloader](https://lib.rs/crates/rawloader).
- **`rawler`** (the dnglab project). LGPL-2.1. Explicitly supports **CR3** among 20+ manufacturers per the dnglab README's format table (Canon CR3/CR2/CRW, Nikon NEF/NRW, Sony ARW/SR2/SRF, Fujifilm RAF, and more). Source: [dnglab/dnglab](https://github.com/dnglab/dnglab). **Actively maintained** — latest published version 0.7.2, February 26, 2026. Source: [lib.rs/crates/rawler](https://lib.rs/crates/rawler). Caveats from the project's own README: dnglab (and by extension rawler) "is currently in alpha state, so don't expect a polished and bugfree application"; the `rawler` crate's "API is not yet stable and thus rawler is not following SemVer"; and the maintainers explicitly prioritize "maintainable, debuggable code" over panic-freedom on malformed input, i.e. they flag it as **not hardened against untrusted/malicious RAW files** — worth noting since LensLocker's managed store ingests arbitrary user files. Source: [dnglab/dnglab README](https://github.com/dnglab/dnglab).

**Net:** `rawler` is the stronger pure-Rust option today (actively maintained, broader/newer format coverage including CR3) despite its pre-1.0 API instability and malformed-input caveat; `rawloader` is safer API-wise (stable, widely used historically, e.g. by `imageflow`/older tools) but stale and missing CR3.

## 4. AVIF

**dav1d and libavif licenses are clean and permissive.** `dav1d` (VideoLAN/AOM-sponsored AV1 decoder) is **BSD-2-Clause**. `libavif` (AOMediaCodec's AVIF codec library) is also **BSD-licensed**. Source: [jbkempf.com, Introducing dav1d](https://jbkempf.com/blog/Introducing-dav1d/), [VideoLAN dav1d project page](https://www.videolan.org/projects/dav1d.html), [Skia mirror of libavif README](https://skia.googlesource.com/external/github.com/AOMediaCodec/libavif/+/refs/heads/main/README.md).

**AV1's patent story is fundamentally different from HEVC's.** The Alliance for Open Media (AOM) operates a **reciprocal royalty-free patent license** (AOMedia Patent License 1.0): each implementer gets "a non-sublicensable, perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, no-charge, royalty-free, irrevocable patent license to its Necessary Claims to make, use, sell, offer for sale, import or distribute any Implementation," in exchange for granting the same back. Source: [Alliance for Open Media, Wikipedia summary of the patent policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alliance_for_Open_Media); see also [IP Europe's critical counter-analysis, "Royalty-free standards are not free of costs: AV1 as a case study"](https://ipeurope.org/blog/royalty-free-standards-are-not-free-of-costs-av1-as-a-case-study/) for the dissenting view that this is a grant/commitment, not a certification of zero third-party-patent risk. Treat as low-risk, not zero-risk.

**The `image` crate's actual current AVIF behavior (verified against live Cargo.toml, not assumed):**
- Default feature set includes `avif` (part of `default-formats`), which pulls in **`ravif`** (pure-Rust, `rav1e`-based) — but `ravif` is an **encoder**. Confirmed directly from the crate's own AVIF decoder module: `src/codecs/avif/decoder.rs` imports `dav1d::{PixelLayout, PlanarImageComponent}` and calls `dav1d::Decoder::new()` — the decode path requires the **`avif-native`** feature (`dep:mp4parse`, `dep:dav1d`), which is **not** in the default feature set and pulls in the **C** `dav1d` library (needing pkg-config/meson/ninja/vcpkg to build on Windows). Source: [image-rs/image Cargo.toml (main branch, fetched live)](https://github.com/image-rs/image/blob/main/Cargo.toml), [image-rs/image src/codecs/avif/decoder.rs](https://github.com/image-rs/image/blob/main/src/codecs/avif/decoder.rs).
- There is an **open, unmerged proposal** to switch decode to **`re_rav1d`** — a fork (by the Rerun project) that makes `dav1d-rs`'s bindings talk to **`rav1d`** (memorysafety/rav1d, a pure-Rust, mostly-safe port of dav1d created via c2rust, BSD-2-Clause) instead of the C library, exposing an API "fully compatible with the dav1d crate." Source: [image-rs/image#2621, "enable re_rav1d by default for avif decoding"](https://github.com/image-rs/image/issues/2621), [memorysafety/rav1d](https://github.com/memorysafety/rav1d), [rerun-io/rerun#7575](https://github.com/rerun-io/rerun/issues/7575). As of this research, this is **not yet merged upstream**.

**Practical implication for LensLocker:** to get AVIF decode without a C toolchain in the build (matching the "statically bundles everything, no runtime downloads" and Windows-first-portable-core goals), LensLocker needs a `[patch.crates-io] dav1d = { git = "...", package = "re_rav1d" }`-style override today, rather than relying on the `image` crate's stock `avif-native` feature. This is a small amount of Cargo plumbing, not a licensing problem — both `dav1d` and `rav1d`/`re_rav1d` are BSD-2-Clause.

## 5. JPEG XL

**`jxl-oxide`** is a pure-Rust, spec-conforming JPEG XL **decoder**, dual-licensed **MIT / Apache-2.0**, with no mandatory C/C++ dependency (an optional `lcms2` feature pulls in a C ICC library; the default/alternative `moxcms` path is pure Rust). Source: [tirr-c/jxl-oxide](https://github.com/tirr-c/jxl-oxide), [jxl-oxide crates.io](https://crates.io/crates/jxl-oxide). This is directly reusable and low-risk for **decode**.

**Encoding is the gap**, already surfaced in [ticket 008's recompression research](recompression.md): the only mature JXL encoders with JPEG-bitstream-reconstruction capability are **`jpegxl-rs`** (GPL-3.0-or-later FFI to libjxl) or shelling out to the libjxl `cjxl` CLI binary — no permissively-licensed, pure-Rust, production-ready JXL encoder exists yet. This doesn't block *importing* .jxl files (decode-only is enough for that), but it does block using JXL as a LensLocker-chosen recompression target without accepting either a GPL dependency or a subprocess dependency on libjxl's own pre-1.0 binaries.

**Patent status:** JPEG XL is characterized as royalty-free based on **Type 1 (royalty-free) patent declarations** submitted to ISO/IEC by Google and Cloudinary, the format's principal designers, granting a "perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, no-charge, royalty-free, irrevocable patent license." Source: search-aggregated from multiple secondary sources including [Wikipedia, JPEG XL](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/JPEG_XL) and the [JPEG XL PATENTS file mirrored in ImageMagick's jpeg-xl fork](https://github.com/ImageMagick/jpeg-xl/blob/main/PATENTS) (a mirror of the upstream libjxl PATENTS grant — worth re-fetching directly from `libjxl/libjxl/PATENTS` for the primary text before this becomes load-bearing; flagged as a gap). Same caveat as AV1: this is a grant from the known major contributors, not a certification that no unrelated third party holds a relevant patent.

## 6. SVG

**`resvg`** (now under the linebender org) plus its core dependencies — **`usvg`** (SVG preprocessor), **`tiny-skia`** (a Rust port of a Skia subset for 2D rendering), and **`fontdb`** (font database) — are all part of the same project and dual-licensed **Apache-2.0 OR MIT**. The project's own README claims distinction as "the only [SVG library] written completely in Rust... There is no non-Rust code in the final binary," with the CLI binary under 3MB and no external runtime dependencies. Source: [linebender/resvg README](https://github.com/linebender/resvg), [resvg crates.io](https://crates.io/crates/resvg). No patent, copyleft, or build-toolchain concerns identified — this is the cleanest family in this survey.

## 7. Open Questions

1. **HEIC/HEIF needs real legal review**, not just this survey, before any decision to bundle even a "detect-and-delegate-to-Windows" strategy ships — confirm with counsel that calling the OS-provided WIC/Media Foundation HEVC codec (rather than bundling a decoder) fully avoids HEVC patent-licensing obligations for LensLocker as the app vendor, not just for Microsoft/the OEM.
2. **mpegla.com and jpeg.org were unreachable** during this research pass (connection refused / not independently re-verified for JPEG XL's PATENTS file) — the MPEG LA royalty figures ($0.20/unit, 100k free, $25M cap) and JPEG XL's primary PATENTS grant text should be re-fetched directly from the primary source before being treated as final for a legal sign-off, rather than relied on via secondary summaries as done here.
3. **IrfanView and XnView's specific licensing/legal rationale for their HEIC approaches were not found in primary statements** from either vendor (both are closed-source, so their risk tolerance/legal posture may not transfer to LensLocker's open posture anyway) — flagged as a gap rather than pursued further, since neither is a governing precedent for an open-source-adjacent offline tool.
4. **`rawler`'s pre-1.0/non-SemVer status and its README's own "not hardened against malformed input" caveat** need a decision: pin a specific commit/tag and fuzz-test LensLocker's import path against malformed RAW files, given the managed store's "verify before taking custody" posture (ticket 005).
5. **Confirm the `[patch]`-onto-`re_rav1d` approach for AVIF decode builds cleanly and statically on Windows** before locking it into the v1 format matrix — this research established licensing/feasibility on paper but did not attempt an actual build.
6. **If LibRaw (C) is ever pulled in as a RAW fallback**, get explicit sign-off on the CDDL-1.0 option's attribution requirements and confirm vcpkg/static-linking mechanics on Windows mirror the CDDL analysis here (this research did not find a maintained Rust LibRaw binding as actively developed as `rawler` itself, which is part of why `rawler` is the primary recommendation over LibRaw).
